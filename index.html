Our Research

Our research interests span Generative AI, Business Optimization, Entrepreneurial Ecosystems, Edge Computing, Mobility Analytics, and Intelligent Transportation Systems.

Pioneering Digital Twins and Mobility

Adapting traffic simulation for Sub-Saharan African cities, integrating Informal Public Transport (IPT) dynamics to optimize transit efficiency and urban mobility planning.

Digital Twins Urban Mobility Traffic Optimization

Federated Computing and Graph Theory

Applying graph theory principles to build scalable and robust federated computing solutions. Exploring data privacy while training powerful AI models.

Federated Learning Graph Theory Privacy

Gen AI and Applications

Exploring the transformative capabilities of Generative AI across diverse domains, from agentic deployment patterns to complex problem-solving in real-world scenarios.

Generative AI ML Applications AI Agents

Operational Excellence & Digital Transformation

Enhancing CMU-Africa's operations through the integration of Business Process Automation, Data Analytics, and AI. Our research focuses on developing intelligent systems that streamline workflows, enable data-driven decision-making, and create a more agile and innovative operational environment.

Process Automation Data Analytics AI Systems

Publications

Our latest research contributions.

2025 Entrepreneurship

Unveiling Entrepreneurial Success: Generative AI Insights into African Startup Founders

Authors: Roy Esibe, Muhammad Aliyu, Jesse Thornburg, João Barros, Erik Stam

Uses Generative AI and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to study the journeys of founders from African unicorn startups, identifying success factors such as mentorship, diaspora networks, and strategic pivots.

2025 Security
PDF

Network Hexagons Under Attack: Secure Crowdsourcing of Georeferenced Data

Authors: Okemawo Obadofin, João Barros

•

IEEE Security and Privacy Workshops (SPW)

Develops the Nexagon protocol for crowdsourcing geospatial data from connected vehicles while preserving privacy and anonymity. Incorporates ephemeral IDs, hardware-backed attestation, and pseudonym certificates.

2025 Computer Vision
DOI

Eyes and Ears: Automated Annotation of Audio Data Using Computer Vision

Authors: Galane Basha Namomsa, Alex Gichamba, Brian Ebiyau, João Barros

•

IEEE International Conference on Image Processing (ICIP)

Introduces a low-cost method to annotate audio by pairing YOLOv8 computer vision detections with sound data. Applied to traffic monitoring, achieving 26x speed-up compared to manual annotation.
